# Tiger Claw Pitch Deck Website — Design Brainstorm

## Context
A cinematic pitch deck website for the Tiger Claw Super Bowl ad "While You Were Sleeping." The site must feel like a film production bible — dark, cinematic, immersive — while presenting the five-act story, scene breakdowns, production notes, and strategic rationale.

## Idea 1: "Noir Storyboard" — Film Noir Meets Editorial Design

**Design Movement:** Neo-Noir Cinema meets Swiss Editorial Typography

**Core Principles:**
1. High-contrast black-and-white with a single accent color (Tiger Claw orange)
2. Typography as architecture — massive type creates spatial hierarchy
3. Negative space as dramatic tension — the emptiness between sections feels intentional
4. Content reveals through scroll like a film reel unwinding

**Color Philosophy:** Near-black (#0A0A0A) backgrounds with warm cream (#F5F0E8) text and Tiger Claw orange (#FF6B00) as the sole accent. The orange appears sparingly — like a match struck in darkness — only on key interactive elements and the Tiger Claw branding. The palette evokes the teal-and-orange color grade described in the ad's style directive.

**Layout Paradigm:** Full-viewport vertical scroll with asymmetric text placement. Scene descriptions sit in narrow columns offset to the left or right, with massive cinematic stills bleeding edge-to-edge. The layout alternates between tight editorial columns and expansive visual moments.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Anamorphic letterbox bars (2.35:1 aspect ratio frames) around scene imagery
2. A persistent timeline scrubber along the left edge showing the 60-second ad progression
3. Film grain texture overlay on all imagery

**Interaction Philosophy:** Scroll-driven reveals — content fades and slides in as if being projected. No clicks required for the main narrative; the entire pitch is experienced as a continuous scroll.

**Animation:** Parallax depth on scene images. Text enters with a typewriter-style stagger. Scene transitions use a horizontal wipe effect reminiscent of film editing. The timeline scrubber pulses with orange at the current section.

**Typography System:** Display: "Playfair Display" at extreme weights (900) for act titles. Body: "Source Serif 4" for scene descriptions (literary, cinematic). UI/Labels: "JetBrains Mono" for technical specs and timestamps.

## Idea 2: "The Director's Deck" — Brutalist Cinema Presentation

**Design Movement:** Brutalist Web Design meets Cinematic Storyboarding

**Core Principles:**
1. Raw, unapologetic structure — exposed grid lines, visible section numbers
2. Monumental scale — oversized typography that dominates the viewport
3. Functional beauty — every element serves the pitch, nothing decorative
4. Tension between stillness and motion — static layouts with micro-animations

**Color Philosophy:** Pure black (#000000) canvas with stark white (#FFFFFF) typography. Tiger Claw orange (#E8620A) used exclusively for data points, numbers, and the CTA. A deep charcoal (#1A1A1A) for card surfaces. The palette is deliberately austere — the content and imagery provide all the visual richness.

**Layout Paradigm:** Horizontal scroll for the five-act story (like swiping through a storyboard), with vertical scroll for deep-dive sections. Each act occupies a full viewport width. The structure mimics a physical storyboard pinned to a wall.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Oversized act numbers ("01", "02") rendered at 30vw as watermarks behind content
2. Dotted grid lines visible in the background, like a director's planning board
3. Scene descriptions presented in monospace blocks resembling script pages

**Interaction Philosophy:** Horizontal swipe for narrative flow, vertical scroll for detail. Hover states reveal additional production notes. The experience feels like flipping through a director's private notebook.

**Animation:** Horizontal slide transitions between acts. Numbers count up when entering the "Why This Ad Wins" section. Subtle parallax on background grid. Text blocks slide in from the edges like script pages being laid on a table.

**Typography System:** Display: "Space Grotesk" at 800 weight for massive act titles. Body: "IBM Plex Mono" for all scene descriptions (script-like). Accent: "Space Grotesk" at 300 weight for supporting text.

## Idea 3: "Midnight Premiere" — Luxury Cinema Experience

**Design Movement:** Art Deco Revival meets Contemporary Luxury Branding

**Core Principles:**
1. Opulence through restraint — rich materials suggested through texture and gradient
2. Vertical rhythm as ceremony — each scroll feels like a curtain rising
3. Gold as earned emphasis — metallic accents appear only at peak moments
4. Immersive atmosphere — the site itself feels like sitting in a darkened theater

**Color Philosophy:** Deep midnight blue (#0C1222) as the primary background, evoking a theater before the lights go down. Warm gold (#C9A84C) for accent lines and key data. Soft silver (#B8C4D4) for body text. Tiger Claw orange (#FF6B00) reserved exclusively for the brand mark and CTA. The palette transitions from cool midnight tones in the opening to warm golden tones in the transformation acts, mirroring the ad's own color arc.

**Layout Paradigm:** Single-column, full-bleed vertical scroll. Each section is a "scene" that fills the viewport. Content is centered with generous margins, creating a theatrical frame. Scene imagery is presented in widescreen letterbox format, floating in the dark space like a screen in a theater.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin gold horizontal rules that extend and retract as dividers between acts
2. A floating "Now Showing" header that updates with the current act title
3. Subtle vignette darkening at viewport edges, simulating theater lighting

**Interaction Philosophy:** Scroll is the only interaction — the pitch unfolds like a private screening. No navigation menu; the experience is linear and intentional. A minimal progress indicator (thin gold line at top) shows position.

**Animation:** Fade-up reveals with slight scale (1.02 → 1.0) for a "focusing" effect. Gold accent lines animate from center outward. Scene images have a subtle Ken Burns zoom. The dollar counter in the strategy section animates from $0 to $1,247,893.41.

**Typography System:** Display: "Cormorant Garamond" at 700 weight — elegant, cinematic, with high contrast strokes. Body: "Libre Baskerville" for scene descriptions — classic, readable, literary. UI: "Outfit" at 300 weight for labels and technical specs.

## Selected Approach: Idea 3 — "Midnight Premiere"

The Midnight Premiere concept best captures the cinematic gravitas of the Tiger Claw Super Bowl ad pitch. Its theater-like atmosphere, the midnight-to-gold color arc that mirrors the ad's own transformation narrative, and the ceremonial scroll experience create the feeling of attending a private screening of something extraordinary. This is the approach that will make someone reading this pitch feel like they are watching the ad, not just reading about it.
